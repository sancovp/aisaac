# Direct Marketing Ascension

# No B.S. Direct Marketing: Ascension is the Best Form of Retention

## The Principle

**It's easier to sell more to existing customers than to acquire new ones.**

Dan Kennedy: "Ascension is the best form of retention. Get customers to buy more from you, and they'll stay with you forever."

## Why Ascension Beats Retention

| Retention Tactics | Ascension Tactics |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Loyalty programs | Higher-tier products |
| Communication/engagement | Premium services |
| Customer service | Exclusive access |
| Discounts | Results-focused upsells |

Discount-based retention trains customers to wait for sales. Ascension-based retention increases value AND loyalty.

## The Ascension Path

### Entry Level
Low-priced, easy-to-buy products that deliver quick wins

### Mid Level
Higher-priced offerings that solve bigger problems

### Premium Level
High-ticket services, done-for-you, coaching

### Continuity
Ongoing retainer, subscription, or membership

## Rules of Ascension

1. **Always overdeliver at current level** — Earn the right to sell more
2. **Solve the next problem** — Don't upsell unrelated products
3. **Create natural progression** — Each purchase should lead to the next logical step
4. **Time it right** — After a success is the best moment to offer more

## Application

1. Map your ascension path (entry → mid → premium → continuity)
2. Identify the natural next purchase after each product
3. Create "done-for-you" versions of "do-it-yourself" products
4. Track customer lifetime value, not just first purchase

**The goal: Make your customer so successful at each level that upgrading feels obvious.**
