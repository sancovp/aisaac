# Direct Marketing Carnegie Secret

# No B.S. Direct Marketing: The Carnegie Secret

## The Core Principle

Dale Carnegie: "Make the other person feel important."

Dan Kennedy adapted this for direct marketing: **Make your prospect feel like they're the ONLY person you're talking to.**

## Why It Works

People ignore mass communication. They respond to personal communication.

- Mass ad: "Dear Customer..."
- Direct marketing: "Dear [Name]..." or even better, content that feels like it was written FOR THEM specifically

## The Application in Copy

### Wrong Approach
```
"We help businesses increase profits."
```

### Right Approach
```
"If you're a local service business owner who's tried advertising but can't figure out why leads aren't converting — this is for you."
```

## The "You" Density Rule

The more times you use "you" and "your" in your copy, the more personal it feels.

| Poor Copy | Good Copy |
|-----------|----------|
| "Our system helps businesses" | "Your business will get more customers" |
| "We offer solutions" | "You'll discover exactly what's costing you sales" |

## Techniques

1. **Segment your list** — Send different messages to different groups
2. **Use their language** — Mirror their words back to them
3. **Address their specific situation** — "If you're struggling with X..."
4. **Make it feel written FOR them** — Even if it's mass distributed

## The Test

Read your copy aloud. Does it sound like you're talking to ONE person? Or does it sound like a broadcast?

If it sounds like a broadcast, rewrite it.
