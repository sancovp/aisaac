# Direct Marketing Follow Up

# direct-marketing-follow-up

## Core Principle

> "One touch rarely converts. You must have structured, systematic follow-up."

**The key insight:** Most sales happen after 5-12 touches. Most marketers give up after 1-2. The money is in the follow-up.

---

## The Four-Step Follow-Up Campaign

### Step 1: Restate, Resell, Remind
Reconnect with the prospect by:
- Restating the core offer
- Reselling the benefits they showed interest in
- Reminding them of urgency/timeline

### Step 2: Present New Information
Share something new:
- A testimonial or case study
- New proof or evidence
- A different angle on the problem

### Step 3: Make a New Irresistible Offer
The third touch should include:
- A modified offer (different price, terms, or packaging)
- New urgency or scarcity
- A fresh reason to respond NOW

### Step 4: Close or Prune
- Attempt the final close
- Prune non-responders from the list
- Move engaged leads to a different sequence

---

## The Closed Loop System

### What It Is
A multi-channel follow-up system that keeps prospects engaged until they buy or exhaust all options.

### Example Structure (42 contacts)
```
Email #1 → Direct Mail #1 → Landing Page Visit → Retargeting
    ↓
Email #2-5 → Phone Call #1 → Another Landing Page
    ↓
Email #6-10 → Phone Call #2 → SMS
    ↓
[Continue until purchase or exhaustion]
```

### The Timing Pattern
Contacts are timed for days 4, 6, 9, 12, etc. — systematic, not random.

---

## The No-Fail Follow-Up Formula

### Key Elements
1. **Multiple channels** — Email, phone, direct mail, retargeting, SMS
2. **Escalating commitment** — Each step asks for slightly more
3. **Fresh reasons** — New offers, new proof, new urgency each touch
4. **Systematic timing** — Built into the calendar, not forgotten

### The 5-12 Touch Rule
> "Most sales happen between touch 5 and touch 12. Most marketers quit at touch 2."

---

## Hole-Plugging Follow-Up

### The Concept
Every customer has an "expiration date" (when they should return). Set alarms for:

| Business Type | Expected Return Interval |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Dentist | Every 6 months |
| Gym | Every 30 days |
| Auto dealer | Every 3 years |
| Clothing store | Before each season |

### The Alarm System
When a customer passes their expiration date without returning:
1. Trigger follow-up sequence
2. Multiple attempts via different channels
3. Offer incentives to return
4. Eventually prune from active list

## Follow-Up Campaign Templates

### Template A: Simple 4-Touch
```
Day 1: Initial contact + offer
Day 4: Follow-up email (restate)
Day 9: Phone call (new proof)
Day 14: Final email (urgent close)
```

### Template B: Complex Multi-Channel
```
Week 1: Email sequence (3 emails) + retargeting ads
Week 2: Phone call + direct mail piece
Week 3: Email sequence (2 emails) + SMS
Week 4: Final phone call + escalating urgency offer
```

### Template C: The 42-Contact Campaign
- 21 emails
- 11 offline contacts (mail, phone)
- 2 online presentations
- 14 SMS messages
- Over 11 weeks

## Automation Opportunities

### What Can Be Automated
- Email sequences (auto-responders)
- Retargeting ads (behavior triggers)
- Phone scripts (but not the calls themselves)
- SMS follow-up (timed triggers)
- Calendar reminders for calls

### The Key
> "It can all be automated." — but the strategy and creative still need human design.

## Key Questions for Follow-Up

/prompts:

- "How many touches am I currently making before giving up?"
- "What's my 4-step follow-up sequence for new leads?"
- "What channels am I using? (Email, phone, retargeting, SMS)"
- "Do I have a closed loop system or just random follow-ups?"
- "What's my customer 'expiration date' and alarm system?"
- "How can I automate my follow-up without losing personalization?"
- "What new offer or proof can I present on touch 3?"
