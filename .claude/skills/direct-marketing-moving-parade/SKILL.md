# Direct Marketing Moving Parade

# No B.S. Direct Marketing: Moving Parade of Interest

## The Concept

Dan Kennedy's "Moving Parade of Interest" — you must keep your marketing consistently visible to prospects until they're ready to buy.

**"The sale is not made at the moment of purchase. It's made months before when the prospect first becomes aware of you."**

## Why Consistency Matters

Most prospects aren't ready to buy the first time they see you. They need:
- Multiple exposures
- Different angles
- Building of trust
- Answering objections over time

**The average prospect needs 5-12 touches before buying.**

## The Parade Metaphor

Your marketing is a parade passing in front of your prospect's house. They might:
- Miss it entirely (first time)
- Notice it but keep walking (second time)
- Stop to watch (third time)
- Join the parade (purchase)

**Each touch is another float in the parade passing by.**

## How to Build Your Parade

| Touch | Format | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Blog post/video | Awareness |
| 2 | Social media | Engagement |
| 3 | Lead magnet | Capture |
| 4 | Email sequence | Nurture |
| 5 | Webinar | Present |
| 6 | Case study | Social proof |
| 7 | Limited offer | Urgency |
| 8+ | Follow-up | Convert |

## The Key Rules

1. **Be consistent** — Sporadic marketing = sporadic results
2. **Vary the message** — Different angles, same promise
3. **Respect the prospect** — Don't spam, but don't go silent
4. **Track what works** — Some touches convert better

## Application

1. Map your current "touches" — how many ways do prospects interact with you?
2. Identify gaps — where do people fall off?
3. Create content that keeps the parade moving
4. Stay visible until they're ready
