# Direct Marketing Success Ratio

# No B.S. Direct Marketing: The Success Ratio

## The Framework

Dan Kennedy's Success Ratio describes typical response rates across marketing channels:

| Channel | Typical Response |
|---------|----------------|
| Cold traffic (paid ads) | 1% |
| Warm audience (email list) | 4% |
| Hot audience (buyers) | 15% |
| Very hot (past buyers, referrals) | 60% |
| Personal network | 20% |

## Why This Matters

Most marketers spend too much trying to convert cold traffic when they should be:
1. Building lists of warm/hot prospects
2. Maximizing value from existing buyers
3. Creating referral mechanisms

## The Hierarchy

```
Cold (1%) → Warm (4%) → Hot (15%) → Very Hot (60%)
     ↑                ↑            ↑
   Ads            Email       Buyers/Referrals
```

## Application

### When Planning Campaigns

1. Don't expect 15% conversion from cold traffic
2. Plan multiple touches (5-12 touches for cold)
3. Budget for attrition at cold stages
4. Maximize hot audience marketing

### Channel Strategy

| If you want... | Use... |
|---------------|--------|
| Volume | Cold traffic (paid ads) |
| Efficiency | Warm traffic (content, lead magnets) |
| Conversion | Hot traffic (buyers, referrals) |

## The Key Insight

"The money is in the list." Your most valuable marketing asset is your list of buyers and hot prospects — not cold traffic.
