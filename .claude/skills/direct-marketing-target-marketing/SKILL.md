# Direct Marketing Target Marketing

# No B.S. Direct Marketing: List Selection & Target Marketing

## The Principle

In direct marketing, the LIST is more important than the CREATIVE.

You can have the best-written sales letter ever created, but if you send it to the wrong list, you'll get nothing.

**The right message to the wrong people = failure.
The right message to the right people = success.**

## List Selection Criteria

### Who to Target

1. **Prior buyers of similar products** — These people have proven they buy
2. **Subscribers to related content** — They've shown interest in the space
3. **Members of relevant groups** — Aligned with your market
4. **Referral sources** — People who connect buyers to products

### Who NOT to Target

- Broad "interested in X" audiences
- Cold audiences with no buying history
- People who consume but never buy

## The RFM Model

| Factor | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **R**ecency | How recently did they buy? |
| **F**requency | How often do they buy? |
| **M**onetary | How much do they spend? |

Best customers: High R, High F, High M

## Target Marketing vs Mass Marketing

| Mass Marketing | Direct/Target Marketing |
|---------------|----------------------|
| "Everyone should see this" | "Only the right people see this" |
| High waste | Low waste |
| Hard to measure | Easy to measure |
| Brand building | Direct response |

## Application

1. Identify who has bought similar products/services
2. Build lists of people who consume related content
3. Use multiple targeting criteria together
4. Test small before scaling
5. Double down on what works

**Rule:** In direct marketing, the quality of your list determines the ceiling of your results.
