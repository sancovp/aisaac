# Hormozi Bonuses

# hormozi-bonuses

## Core Principle

> "A single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into its component parts and stacked as bonuses."

**Key insight:** Don't discount. Add bonuses instead.

---

## The Psychology of Bonus Stacking

**How infomercials work:**
1. Establish the price
2. Say "But wait, there's more!"
3. Stack bonus after bonus
4. Each bonus expands the value gap until it feels stupid to say no

**The anchor:** Price is anchored to the core offer. Each bonus makes the gap wider.

---

## The Rule: Never Discount the Main Offer

**Why?**
- Discounting teaches customers prices are negotiable
- Once discounted, it's hard to go back
- Discounting signals weakness

**The alternative:** Add bonuses that increase perceived value without changing the core price.

---

## Bonus Presentation: 101 vs Group Selling

### One-on-One (101 Selling):
1. Ask for the sale first
2. If they say YES → Reveal bonuses as "wow experience"
3. If they say NO → Present a bonus that matches their obstacle, then ask again

### Group Selling:
- Present all bonuses upfront
- Each bonus is a reason to say yes
- Layer them until the gap is too big to reject

## The 11 Bonus Rules

| # | Rule | Description |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | Always offer them | Use the bullet bundle you built |
| 2 | Give them benefit names | Name bonuses with the outcome they provide |
| 3 | Explain the connection | HOW it relates to their issue |
| 4 | Provide proof | Stats, testimonials, case studies |
| 5 | Paint vivid picture | What their life looks like after using it |
| 6 | Always price them | Put a dollar value on each bonus |
| 7 | Tools > Trainings | Checklists and tools have lower effort = higher value |
| 8 | Address obstacles | Each bonus should neutralize a specific objection |
| 9 | Solve next problems | Address what they'll need AFTER they buy |
| 10 | Value > Core offer | Total bonus value should eclipse core offer value |
| 11 | Add scarcity/urgency | Put time limits on bonuses themselves |

## The Bonus Construction Process

### Step 1: List All Deliverables
From your value stack, list everything you COULD deliver.

### Step 2: Group by Delivery Method
```
ONE-ON-ONE (done-for-you)
  - In-person experience
  - Personalized solution
  - Direct coaching

SMALL GROUP (done-with-you)
  - Group coaching
  - Shared experience
  - Peer learning

ONE-TO-MANY (done-by-you)
  - Recorded training
  - Templates
  - Self-paced course
```

### Step 3: The Delivery Cube

Ask these three questions for each deliverable:

| Question | Options |
|----------|---------|
| **Level of personal attention** | One-on-one / Small group / One-to-many |
| **Level of effort expected** | DIY / Done with you / Done for you |
| **Environment/Medium** | In-person / Virtual / Async / Physical |

### Step 4: Apply Bonus Scarcity

**Quantity scarcity:** "Only people in this program get these bonuses — never available elsewhere"

**Urgency scarcity:** "If you buy today, I'll add X bonus worth $Y for free"

## Advanced: Joint Venture Bonuses

**Get other businesses to contribute bonuses for FREE.**

**How it works:**
1. Approach non-competitors with your ideal clients
2. Offer them exposure to your high-quality customers
3. They give you their product/service as a bonus
4. You get valuable bonuses at zero cost

**Example — Pain Clinic Bundle:**
```
Chiropractor → 2 free adjustments ($100 value)
Massage therapist → 1 free massage ($75 value)
Health food company → 30% discount ($50 value)
Orthotics company → Discount ($150 value)
Local gym → Free training session + month membership ($100 value)
Pharmacy → Monthly discounts ($100 value)

Total bonus value: $575 on top of core offer
```

**The multiplier effect:**
- You can justify a higher price because total value > price
- Negotiate affiliate commissions ON TOP of the donated products
- Creates a win-win-win for you, the partners, and the client

## The "Free Plus" Affiliate Model

Alex did this with their supplement company:

1. Gym owners get 30% discount for their clients (from Prestige Labs)
2. Owners also get 40% commission on all sales netted after discount
3. Result: Clients save money, owners earn commissions, supplements company gets customers

## The Stacking Order

When presenting bonuses, order matters:

1. **Core offer first** — Establish the anchor price
2. **Biggest value bonus** — Peak their interest
3. **Related bonuses** — Build on the momentum
4. **Urgency trigger** — "If you act today..."
5. **Final bonus** — The "one last thing"

## Questions to Ask When Building Bonuses

/prompts:

- "What do my clients need BEFORE they achieve results? (Address those obstacles)"
- "What will they need AFTER they achieve initial results? (Solve next problems)"
- "What are the specific objections that prevent people from saying yes? (Neutralize each)"
- "What tools/checklists could I create that would make their job easier?"
- "Who are non-competitive businesses that serve the same客户? (Joint venture partners)"
- "What would each bonus be worth to the client? (Price everything)"
- "How can I add scarcity or urgency to the bonuses themselves?"
- "Is the total value of bonuses greater than the core offer price?"
