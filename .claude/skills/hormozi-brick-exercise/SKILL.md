# Hormozi Brick Exercise

# hormozi-brick-exercise

## Core Principle

> "Life will pay you for your ability to solve problems using a divergent thought process."

**The key insight:** Most people are trained for convergent thinking (math problems with one answer). But life rewards divergent thinking (many possible solutions to one problem). The Brick Exercise trains your brain for divergent thinking so you can create offers that make you rich.

---

## Convergent vs. Divergent Thinking

### Convergent Thinking (Math Mode)
- Many variables, all known
- Unchanging conditions
- Single correct answer
- Binary: right or wrong
- What school teaches
- Easy to grade

### Divergent Thinking (Creative Mode)
- Multiple variables, known and unknown
- Dynamic conditions
- Multiple possible answers
- One answer may be WAY more right than others
- What life rewards
- Not taught in school

### The Example
**Convergent:** You have 3 salespeople who can each take 100 calls/month. 4 calls = 1 sale. You need 110 sales. How many salespeople?

**Answer:** 5. (Math. Clean. Binary.)

**Divergent:** How many ways can a brick provide value?

**Answer:** Unlimited. (Creative. Multiple valid paths.)

---

## The Brick Exercise

### The Exercise (2 minutes)
1. Set a timer for 120 seconds
2. Think of a brick
3. Write down as many different uses of a brick as possible
4. How many different ways could a brick be used in life to provide value?

### The Point
> "This exercise will engage the part of your brain that you will need to use in order to make something magical."

You're not looking for practical answers. You're forcing your brain to generate MANY options instead of converging on the obvious one.

---

## Why This Matters for Offers

### The Problem
When creating offers, most people default to the obvious solution — the same thing everyone else does. This is convergent thinking applied to business.

### The Opportunity
Divergent thinking reveals:
- Options they've never considered
- Delivery mechanisms they didn't know existed
- Combinations that create massive value

### The Result
> "What could you do that someone would immediately say, all that? Seriously? I'm in."

---

## How to Run the Exercise

### For Each Problem Your Client Has:
1. **Set timer:** 120 seconds
2. **Write the problem:** What problem does your customer have?
3. **Brainstorm:** List every possible solution, no matter how weird
4. **Don't filter:** Include impractical, expensive, time-consuming ideas
5. **Go for quantity:** The more ideas, the more likely you find the gem

### The Brick Exercise Applied
Think of ONE component of your offer, then ask:
- What could a brick be? (Gold brick? Plastic brick? Lego brick? Brick with holes?)
- How many different uses?

Apply the same logic to your offer components.

## Example: Grocery Shopping Problem

### Convergent (Obvious)
"Offer a grocery shopping service."

### Divergent (Many Options)

**DIY:**

- Meal planning system
- Grocery calculator tool

**Done-With-You:**
- In-person grocery tour
- Live video grocery walk-through
- One-on-one grocery coaching call

**Done-For-You:**
- Full-service grocery delivery
- Pre-made Instacart carts
- Personal grocery shopper

**Each option creates a different offer at a different price point.**

## The Framework for Offer Creation

### Step 1: Identify Problems
List all problems your customer has before, during, and after using your product/service.

### Step 2: Apply Brick Exercise
For EACH problem:
1. Set timer
2. List ALL possible solutions (divergent)
3. Include impractical ones
4. Include expensive ones
5. Include weird combinations

### Step 3: Filter Later
After you've exhausted options:
- Which are actually deliverable?
- Which create massive value?
- Which can you price appropriately?

### Step 4: Stack into Grand Slam Offer
Combine the best components from your divergent lists.
