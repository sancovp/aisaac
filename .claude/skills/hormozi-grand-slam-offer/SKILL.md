# Hormozi Grand Slam Offer

# hormozi-grand-slam-offer

## Core Definition

> "Make people an offer so good, they would feel stupid saying no." — TJ (Travis Jones), the quote that changed Alex's life

The **Grand Slam Offer** is the proper combination of pricing, value, guarantees, and naming that makes your offer irresistible.

**Key insight:** It takes no more effort to make a Grand Slam offer than to strike out. The difference is skill and alignment with your audience's desires.

---

## The Grand Slam Offer Formula

```
GRAND SLAM OFFER = High Price + Massive Value + Strong Guarantee + Clear Naming
                   ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                   Where: Value >> Price (they feel stupid saying no)
```

---

## The 5-Phase Grand Slam Offer Process

### Phase 1: Identify the Avatar
Find your ideal customer with 4 criteria:
1. **Massive pain** — Desperately need what you're offering
2. **Purchasing power** — Have money or access to money
3. **Easy to target** — Can find them (associations, groups, lists)
4. **Growing market** — Market not shrinking

**Rule:** "Don't niche-slap me." — Commit to one niche until you find the Grand Slam offer. Most quit too early.

---

### Phase 2: Set the Price (Charge What It's Worth)

**The Virtuous Cycle of Premium Pricing:**

| When You RAISE Prices | When You LOWER Prices |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| ↑ Client emotional investment | ↓ Client emotional investment |
| ↑ Perceived value | ↓ Perceived value |
| ↑ Client results | ↓ Client results |
| ↑ Best clients (easiest to satisfy) | ↑ Worst clients (never satisfied) |
| ↑ Margin to invest in systems | ↓ Margin to invest in anything |
| WIN | LOSE |

**The Pricing Pyramid:**
```
Premium Price → More value delivered → Better clients → Better results → WIN
Budget Price → Less value perceived → Worst clients → Worse results → LOSE
```

**Rule:** You should charge so much it "hurts a little" when they pay. The more invested, the more they follow through, the better results.

---

### Phase 3: Build the Value Stack

The **Value Equation:**
```
VALUE = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood)
        ─────────────────────────────────────
         (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**Maximize top (increase value):**
- Make the Dream Outcome bigger/more aspirational
- Add proof (social proof, case studies, guarantees)

**Minimize bottom (reduce friction):**
- Compress time to results (immediate wins)
- Remove required actions (done-for-you elements)

**The Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum:**
```
Easy to SELL ←─────────────────────→ Easy to FULFILL
(hard to deliver)                   (hard to sell)
     ↓                                    ↓
  Less work                          More work
  per client                         per client
     ↓                                    ↓
 Scale fast                         Scale slow
 Risk: quality                      Risk: demand
```

**Alex's Mantra:** "Create flow, monetize flow, then add friction."

Start by over-delivering (done-for-you), then systematize to reduce your workload while maintaining value.

---

### Phase 4: Enhance with Components

**4a. Guarantee**
- Remove all risk from the prospect
- Types: Money-back, performance-based, "Don't achieve result? We'll fix it free"
- The stronger the guarantee, the more you can charge

**4b. Bonuses**
- Break your deliverable into stacked components
- Present them as "But wait, there's more!"
- Psychology: Each bonus adds to perceived value exponentially
- Rule: "Add bonuses instead of discounting"
- Bonus best practices:
  - Give them benefit-driven names
  - Explain HOW it relates to their problem
  - Provide proof of value
  - Paint vivid mental picture of result
  - Always put a price tag on it
  - Tools/checklists > additional trainings (lower effort = higher value)

**4c. Scarcity & Urgency (4 types)**

| Type | What It Is | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Quantity** | Limited spots/items | "Only 10 spots available" |
| **Timeline** | Time deadline | "Offer ends Friday" |
| **Price Escalation** | Price goes up over time | "Price rises $100 every week" |
| **Exploding Opportunity** | Tick-tock window | "Bonus disappears when timer hits zero" |

---

### Phase 5: Name It (The Wrapper)

The **Headline Formula:**
```
[Modifier] + [Avatar] + [Goal] + [Time] + [Container Word] = Headline
```

| Component | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| **Modifier** | Free, 88% Off, $2,000 Off, Instant |
| **Avatar** | Dentists, Busy Moms, Gym Owners, Real Estate Agents |
| **Goal** | Celebrity Smile, Six-Pack Abs, First 10 Clients |
| **Time** | 30 Days, 6 Weeks, In 90 Days |
| **Container** | Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Masterclass, Sprint, Fast Track |

**Examples:**
- "Free 6-Week Lean By Summer Challenge"
- "$2,000 Off Celebrity Smile for Lakeway Moms"
- "Fill Your Gym in 30 Days — Free Blueprint"
- "7-Figure Agency 12-Week Intensive"

**Pro Tips:**
- **Rhyme:** "Six-pack fast track," "five-day book print sprint"
- **Alliteration:** "Big Booty Bootcamp," "Debt Detox"
- **Name sub-bonuses using same formula**

## The Brick Exercise (Divergent Thinking)

When stuck on what to include in your offer:

1. Set a 2-minute timer
2. Think of a brick
3. Write down as many DIFFERENT uses for a brick as possible
4. This flexes your divergent thinking muscle

**The point:** There are always more solutions than you initially think. Don't default to the obvious.
