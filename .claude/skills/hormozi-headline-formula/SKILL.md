# Hormozi Headline Formula

# hormozi-headline-formula

## Core Principle

> "Like the tree that falls in the forest that no one hears, having a Grand Slam offer will not make you money if no one finds out about it."

**The key insight:** Naming your offer correctly determines how well your advertising converts, how big of a response you get from outbound, and how many inbound responses you get from organic. The Magic Headline Formula gives you a repeatable system.

---

## The M-A-G-I-C Formula

| Letter | Component | What It Is |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **M** | Magnet | Reason why you're running this promotion |
| **A** | Avatar | Who this is for |
| **G** | Goal | Their dream outcome |
| **I** | Interval | Duration/time to expect results |
| **C** | Container | What type of program/package |

**Note:** Use 3-5 components typically. Not all are mandatory. Balance brevity with specificity.

---

## The Components in Detail

### M — Magnet (Reason Why)

Answers: Why are you making this offer? / Why should they respond?

**Examples:**
| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Free/Discount | Free, 88% off, Giveaway |
| Seasonal | Spring, Summer, Back to school, New Year |
| Event | Grand opening, Anniversary, Halloween |
| Change | New management, New building |

### A — Avatar (Who It's For)

Call out your ideal customer specifically. More specific = more resonant.

**Examples:**
- Bee cave dentists
- Rolling hill moms
- Brick and mortar businesses
- Brooklyn busy executives
- Retired athletes

**Pro tip:** In local markets, go hyper-local:
- Not "Baltimore" → "Towson, Maryland"
- Not "Chicago" → "Hinsdale"

### G — Goal (Dream Outcome)

What they want to achieve. Single word, phrase, event, feeling, or experience.

**Examples:**
| Outcome | Goal Words |
|---------|------------|
| Appearance | Celebrity smile, Six-pack, Little black dress |
| Business | Double your profit, First client, Seven figure |
| Health | Pain-free, Never out of breath |
| Lifestyle | High ticket, Hundred K |

**The more specific and tangible, the better.**

### I — Interval (Duration)

How long until they get results.

**Examples:**
- 21 days
- 6 weeks
- 3 months
- 10 minutes
- 2 minutes

**Compliance note:** If you make quantifiable claims (income, weight loss), many platforms won't approve with stated durations. Use intervals where you don't need compliance.

### C — Container (Program Type)

Denotes a bundled system — something that can't be commoditized.

**Examples:**
| Category | Container Words |
|----------|---------------|
| Intense | Challenge, Bootcamp, Intensive, Sprint |
| Strategic | Blueprint, Masterclass, Accelerator, Game plan |
| Transform | Detox, Transformation, Comeback, Reset |
| Creative | Launch, Summit, Meetup, Deep dive |
| Short | Hack, Shortcut, Fast track, Slingshot |

## Pro Tips

### Rhyme Your Program Name
Good rhymes stick in people's minds.

**Examples:**
- Six-pack fast track
- Marriage thrive deep dive
- 12-week two-putt shortcut
- Celebrity butt shortcut

### Alliteration
All words start with same letter/sound.

**Examples:**
- Make money masterclass
- Change your life challenge
- Big booty bootcamp
- Debt detox

**Don't force it. It's a nice-to-have, not a requirement.**

### Name Sub-Items
Use the formula for each bonus and component in your stack. Automatically enhances perceived value.

## The Offer Fatigue Framework

When offers fatigue, vary in this order (lightest to heaviest):

| Order | What to Change | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | **Creative** | New images, videos, hooks |
| 2 | **Body copy** | Different ad copy |
| 3 | **Headline** | New wrapper/name |
| 4 | **Duration** | 6 weeks → 42 days |
| 5 | **Enhancer** | Different free/discount component |
| 6 | **Monetization** | Change entire pricing structure |

**Key:** Most of the time, you only need to change the first few items. Save the big changes for when truly needed.

> "This one concept will get you leads forever. We're simply changing the wrapper."

---

## Compliance Notes

### Quantifiable Claims + Duration
If you state a specific outcome WITH a timeline (e.g., "$10,000 in 10 days"), many platforms won't approve.

**Workaround:** Use duration without quantifiable claim:
- "$10,000 in 10 days" → compliance issue
- "Make your first sale in 10 days" → might be okay
- "10 days to your first sale" → might be okay

### When In Doubt
Test with platform rules. What they allow changes.
