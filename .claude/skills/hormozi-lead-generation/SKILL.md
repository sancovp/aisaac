# Hormozi Lead Generation

# hormozi-lead-generation

## Core Principle

> "It's hard to be poor with leads banging down your door."

**The equation:** More leads → More sales → More money. Advertising gives you endless second chances.

---

## The Lead Generation Framework

**Prerequisite:** You must have a Grand Slam Offer first (see `hormozi-grand-slam-offer`)

**What a lead IS:**
- Someone with the problem you solve
- Someone with the money to pay for your solution
- Someone who is interested in what you're selling

**What a lead is NOT:**
- Just a name on a list
- Someone who clicked your ad but will never buy
- Any person in your target demographic

---

## The Four Core Ways to Get Leads

Alex discovered only FOUR ways exist to generate leads:

| # | Method | Description |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Cold Traffic** | Ads to people who don't know you exist |
| 2 | **Warm Traffic** | Ads to people who know you but aren't customers yet |
| 3 | **Hot Traffic** | Referrals from existing customers/partners |
| 4 | **Earned Traffic** | Organic content, SEO, PR, word of mouth |

---

## The Lead Quality Framework

Not all leads are equal. Alex separates leads into:

### Types of Leads

| Lead Type | Description | Cost | Conversion |
|-----------|-------------|------|-----------|
| **Mass lead** | Everyone who shows interest | Cheap | Low |
| **Marketing lead** | Engaged but not ready | Medium | Medium |
| **Sales lead** | Ready to talk | Expensive | High |
| **Qualified lead** | Ready, willing, able | Most expensive | Highest |

### The Key Question
**"Does this lead have the problem I solve AND the money to pay for it?"**

If yes → Worth your time
If no → Move on

---

## Cold Traffic (Paid Advertising)

### What It Is
Running ads to people who've never heard of you.

### The Math
```
Revenue = Leads × Conversion Rate × Offer Value
```

### Key Metrics
- **CPL** (Cost Per Lead) = What you pay per lead
- **CPA** (Cost Per Acquisition) = What you pay per customer
- **LTV** (Lifetime Value) = What each customer is worth
- **ROAS** (Return on Ad Spend) = Revenue / Ad Spend

### The Rule
> You can only scale if your LTV > CPA

---

## Warm Traffic (Targeted Audience)

### What It Is
Marketing to people who know you but haven't bought yet:
- Email list subscribers
- Social media followers
- People who've visited your site
- People who've engaged with your content

### The Advantage
Warm traffic is 5-10x cheaper than cold traffic because they already trust you somewhat.

---

## Hot Traffic (Referrals)

### What It Is
Customers and partners sending you leads.

### Why It's Best
- Zero cost per lead
- Highest conversion rate
- Customers sell for you

### How to Generate Referrals
1. **Create a remarkable product** — People refer things worth referring
2. **Ask for referrals** — "Who do you know that would benefit?"
3. **Create referral incentives** — Both referrer and referred get value
4. **Build partner networks** — They send you customers, you send them customers

---

## Earned Traffic (Organic)

### What It Is
People finding you through:
- Content marketing (blogs, videos, podcasts)
- SEO (search engine optimization)
- Public relations
- Word of mouth

### The Challenge
Slower to build but compounds over time.

---

## The Lead Machine Concept

**The goal:** Build a machine that generates leads on autopilot.

```
TRAFFIC SOURCE → LEAD CAPTURE → FOLLOW-UP → SALE → REFERRAL
      ↑                                              ↓
      └──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                    (Compounds over time)
```

### The Flywheel Effect
More leads → More sales → More referrals → More leads → ...

---

## The $100M Lead Machine Blueprint

**Alex's framework for building a lead machine:**

```
STEP 1: Have a Grand Slam Offer (prerequisite)
         ↓
STEP 2: Test cold traffic until you find a winning ad + offer combo
         ↓
STEP 3: Scale what's working
         ↓
STEP 4: Build email/follow-up sequence to warm up cold leads
         ↓
STEP 5: Create referral mechanisms for customers
         ↓
STEP 6: Build partner/affiliate channels
         ↓
STEP 7: Layer in content/SEO for earned traffic
         ↓
RESULT: Multiple lead sources working simultaneously
```

## The Lead Quality Score

Before pursuing a lead, score them on:

| Factor | Score 1 | Score 5 |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Problem fit** | Vague problem | Exact problem you solve |
| **Money** | No budget | Has budget/prepared to spend |
| **Timing** | Not thinking about solving | Actively seeking solution |
| **Authority** | Can't decide | Decision maker |
| **Effort** | Will need hand-holding | Self-sufficient |

**Score < 15:** Low priority, nurture with content
**Score 15-20:** Worth a sales call
**Score 20-25:** Priority prospect

---

## Key Metrics to Track

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Conversion Rate** | Leads that become customers | Track over time |
| **Cost Per Lead** | Total ad spend / # of leads | As low as possible |
| **Cost Per Acquisition** | Total ad spend / # of customers | Must be < LTV |
| **Lead Close Rate** | Leads that become sales | Higher is better |
| **LTV:CPA Ratio** | Customer value / Acquisition cost | Want > 3:1 |

---

## Questions to Ask When Building a Lead System

/prompts:

- "Which of the 4 traffic sources am I currently using? Which am I ignoring?"
- "What's my cost per lead and cost per acquisition?"
- "Is my LTV higher than my CPA? If not, how do I increase LTV or lower CPA?"
- "What's my lead quality score? Am I chasing the right leads?"
- "How can I turn cold traffic into warm traffic faster?"
- "What's my referral mechanism? Am I asking for referrals?"
- "How long is my follow-up sequence? (Hint: longer is better)"
- "If I could only focus on ONE traffic source, which would give me the best ROI?"
