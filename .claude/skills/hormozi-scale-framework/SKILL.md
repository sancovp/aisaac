# Hormozi Scale Framework

# hormozi-scale-framework

## Core Principle

> "First, you do way more of the advertising that works until it breaks. Then the next drop-off point becomes obvious. Then you keep that level of advertising up while you go back, fix the constraint, and make it better."

**The key insight:** Don't add new advertising channels until you've exhausted your current ones. Scale through depth before width.

---

## The Three Scaling Strategies

### Strategy 1: More
Do MORE of what's already working.

**The rule:** Scale successful activities until they break or diminishing returns hit.

**How it works:**
- Identify what's working
- Double down on that channel
- Keep increasing until returns decrease

### Strategy 2: Better
Make what's working MORE efficient.

**The rule:** Fix the constraints that limit your successful channels.

**When to use:**
- When scaling more hits diminishing returns
- When a bottleneck appears (creative, offer, delivery)
- Before moving to completely new channels

**Common constraints to fix:**
- Creative quality
- Offer optimization
- Targeting refinement
- Landing page/conversion

### Strategy 3: New
Add NEW advertising channels.

**The rule:** Only after exhausting more AND better do you add new.

**The order of expansion:**
1. New ad placements on familiar platforms
2. New platforms with familiar content
3. New platforms with new content
4. Completely new core four activities

## The Scaling Hierarchy

### The Order of Operations

| Priority | Action | When to Apply |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| **1st** | More | While it's working, scale it |
| **2nd** | Better | When more hits a ceiling |
| **3rd** | New | Only when better is exhausted |

### The Key Question
> "What's stopping them from doing 10 times what they're currently doing?"

---

## Depth vs. Width Strategy

### Depth Then Width (Preferred for most)
```
Maximize Platform A
    ↓
Add Platform B (maintain Platform A)
    ↓
Add Platform C (maintain A and B)
    ↓
Repeat until all relevant platforms maxed
```

**Advantages:**
- Learn one platform deeply before moving on
- Returns compound on known platforms
- Lower risk

### Width Then Depth
```
Try multiple platforms at once
    ↓
See what sticks
    ↓
Double down on winners
    ↓
Go deep on winning platforms
```

**Advantages:**
- Faster to find winning platforms
- Good when you need speed

## The Core Four Activities

These are the four ways to let people know about your stuff:

| Activity | Type | Examples |
|----------|------|----------|
| **Warm Outreach** | 1:1, Known | Personal emails, DMs, calls |
| **Cold Outreach** | 1:1, Unknown | Cold emails, cold calling |
| **Content** | 1:Many, Public | Posts, videos, blogs |
| **Paid Ads** | 1:Many, Paid | Facebook Ads, Google Ads |

### Combining Core Four
> "Every combination of the core four advertising activities boost each other in some way."

- Content + Paid Ads → better ads
- Cold Outreach + Content → better outreach
- All four compound together

## The Scaling Decision Tree

```
Is current advertising working?
    ↓
YES → Can we do MORE? → YES → Scale MORE
    ↓
NO
    ↓
Can we make it BETTER? → YES → Fix constraints
    ↓
NO
    ↓
Can we do it on NEW platform? → YES → Add NEW platform
    ↓
NO
    ↓
Can we do NEW core activity? → YES → Add NEW activity
    ↓
NO
    ↓
Move on (exhausted)
```

## Key Principles

### The Compound Effect
> "The money, the systems, and experience you earn from the prior method will help you master the next one."

### More + Better > New
Most businesses should exhaust more and better before touching new.

### The Question to Ask
> "What will get me the most leads for the least amount of work?"
