# Hormozi Scarcity Urgency

# hormozi-scarcity-urgency

## Core Principle

**Scarcity and urgency create action. Without them, people procrastinate indefinitely.**

---

## The 4 Types of Scarcity and Urgency

| Type | What It Is | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **1. Quantity** | Limited spots/items | "Only 10 spots available" |
| **2. Timeline** | Time deadline | "Offer ends Friday" |
| **3. Price Escalation** | Price goes up over time | "Price rises $100 every week" |
| **4. Exploding Opportunity** | Tick-tock window | "Bonus disappears when timer hits zero" |

---

## Type 1: Quantity Scarcity

### What It Is
Limited availability of spots, items, or access.

### Examples
- "Only 10 people per cohort"
- "Limited to first 50 sign-ups"
- "Only 100 copies of this available"
- "VIP access limited to 20 members"

### When It Works
- When supply is genuinely limited
- When demand is already high (social proof + scarcity = conversion)
- When the number feels achievable but not trivially small

### When It Doesn't Work
- When scarcity isn't credible (anyone can sign up anytime)
- When the limit is so low it seems fake
- When there's no demand to begin with

---

## Type 2: Timeline Urgency

### What It Is
A specific deadline when the offer expires.

### Examples
- "Offer ends Friday at midnight"
- "Sale ends March 31st"
- "This pricing available until April 15th"
- "Enrollment closes on the 28th"

### When It Works
- When the deadline feels real and enforceable
- When the prospect needs a push to act now
- When combined with a compelling offer

### When It Doesn't Work
- When the deadline seems arbitrary
- When the offer isn't compelling enough to warrant urgency
- When deadlines keep getting extended (kills credibility)

---

## Type 3: Price Escalation

### What It Is
The price increases over time — either tomorrow, or as more people join.

### Examples
- "Price goes up $100 every week you wait"
- "Early bird pricing: $499. After Jan 1st: $799"
- "Join now at $1,997. Price increases $200/month"
- "Founding member pricing locks in forever"

### Why It Works
- Creates a "if I don't act now, I pay more later" dynamic
- Ties price to action (inaction = higher cost)
- Makes the current price feel like a deal in comparison

### The Key to Success
**NEVER raise prices without announcing it first.**

> "Never raise your prices without letting people know. It shows a position of strength and will give you a nice little influx of cash from people in the pipeline who are on the fence."

---

## Type 4: Exploding Opportunity

### What It Is
An arbitrage or time-sensitive opportunity where early action = disproportionate gains.

### Examples
- Cryptocurrency trading signals (window of opportunity before market corrects)
- Job offers with signing bonuses that decrease over time
- Real estate deals that disappear if not seized quickly
- Affiliate opportunities before the platform gets saturated

### Why It Works
The opportunity itself has a ticking clock. The longer someone waits, the more they miss out.

**Key distinction:** This isn't artificial urgency — it's a genuine feature of the opportunity.

---

## The Psychology of Urgency

### Why Urgency Works

1. **Loss aversion** — People act more strongly to avoid a loss than to gain something
2. **Procrastination killer** — Gives permission to act now instead of "thinking about it"
3. **Decision accelerator** — Reduces the endless evaluation loop
4. **Social proof amplifier** — "Others are acting, should I?"

### When Urgency Backfires

1. **Fake scarcity** — "Limited time!" but the same offer appears next week
2. **Overuse** — Every offer has urgency = no urgency
3. **Not credible** — The urgency doesn't match the offer's real value
4. **Manipulative feeling** — Can feel pushy if not handled well

---

## The Combination Stack

**Most powerful:** Combine multiple types simultaneously

```
"Only 10 spots available (Quantity)
 at the early bird price of $497 (Price Lock)
 if you sign up before Friday (Timeline)
 or the price jumps to $797 (Escalation)
 and you lose access to the bonus (Exploding)"
```

---

## Ethical Use of Scarcity and Urgency

**The rule:** Scarcity and urgency should be **real**, not manufactured.

| Real Scarcity | Fake Scarcity |
|---------------|---------------|
| "We only have 10 spots per cohort because of our capacity" | "Limited time offer!" but it's always available |
| "Early bird pricing ends on this date" | "Sale ends... sometime... maybe" |
| "Founding member price locks in forever" | "Price going up tomorrow!" (but it never does) |

**Why it matters:** Once you break trust with fake urgency, every future offer is suspect.

---

## The Right Way to Add Urgency

1. **Start with a strong offer** — Urgency amplifies a good offer, can't save a weak one
2. **Make it real** — Only use scarcity that's actually true
3. **Be specific** — "10 spots left" beats "Limited availability"
4. **Communicate it clearly** — Don't bury it in fine print
5. **Follow through** — If you say the price goes up, it must go up

---

## The Order of Application

When promoting an offer, vary these elements in this order when one fatigues:

1. **Creative** — Change images/video in ads
2. **Body copy** — Change the ad text itself
3. **Headline/Wrapper** — Change the offer name or framing
4. **Duration** — Change the time component
5. **Enhancer** — Change the free/discounted component
6. **Monetization structure** — Change the entire pricing model (rare)

---

## Questions to Ask When Adding Scarcity/Urgency

/prompts:

- "What is genuinely scarce about this offer? (Don't manufacture)"
- "What is a credible timeline that creates real urgency?"
- "Is price escalation appropriate? If so, what are the tiers?"
- "Is there an exploding opportunity element to emphasize?"
- "How can I combine 2-3 types for maximum effect?"
- "Is my scarcity/urgency credible? Will I follow through on it?"
- "What's the right amount of urgency — enough to act, not so much it feels fake?"
