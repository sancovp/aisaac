# DOCTOR Closer Script Generator Framework

Medical-framed closing experience. Same psychology as Hormozi's CLOSER but carries authority + urgency of care instead of sales.

## The Acronym

| Letter | Step | What You Do | Dentist Equivalent |
|--------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **D** | DIAGNOSE | Free discovery session. Scan their business. Take x-rays. | X-rays, 3Shape scan |
| **O** | OBSERVE | Show them the problem visually. "See this? This is the leak." | "See this pocket depth? That's 5mm." |
| **C** | CONSEQUENCES | The awful completion of their current trajectory. Leave them with it. | "If we don't treat this, surgery later, much more expensive" |
| **T** | TREAT | Tiered solutions by certainty level. Antibiotics → operation → permanent. | Treatment plan with options |
| **O** | OPTIONS | Financing, payment plans, profit sharing vs retainer. Remove the money objection. | "Here are the financing options" |
| **R** | RESOLVE | Close + reinforce. "You're making the right choice." Schedule it. | "Let's schedule the procedure" |

## CLOSER → DOCTOR Mapping

| CLOSER (Hormozi) | DOCTOR | Why DOCTOR is stronger |
|-------------------|--------|----------------------|
| C - Clarify why they're here | D - Diagnose | You're the expert scanning them, not asking |
| L - Label the problem | O - Observe | They SEE it themselves on the x-ray |
| O - Overview past pain | C - Consequences | Future trajectory is scarier than past pain |
| S - Sell the vacation | T - Treat | Treatment feels like care, not a pitch |
| E - Explain away concerns | O - Options | Financing removes the objection structurally |
| R - Reinforce decision | R - Resolve | "Right choice for your health" > "great purchase" |

## Key Difference

CLOSER feels like sales. DOCTOR feels like care.
The prospect doesn't think "I'm being closed." They think "I'm being treated."

## Usage

Generate a DOCTOR script for any niche:
1. What's the diagnostic? (What do you scan/audit for free?)
2. What's the observation? (What visual proof do you show them?)
3. What are the consequences? (What happens if they do nothing?)
4. What are the treatments? (Tiered by certainty: quick fix → real fix → permanent)
5. What are the options? (How do they pay? What structures?)
6. What's the resolve? (How do you close + reinforce?)
