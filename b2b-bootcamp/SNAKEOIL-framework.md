# SNAKE OIL — The Anti-Positioning Framework

**S**illy **N**oobs **A**ttempting **K**leptocracy **E**verywhere **O**nly **I**nciting you to **L**evel up

## The Principle

It's not about whether or not something IS snake oil. It's about whether or not *from your offer's POV* something else is snake oil. Then you just go steal their customers and laugh your way to the bank like Scrooge McDuck.

## How It Works

Everything is snake oil relative to something better:

```
From ISAAC's POV:     Isabella's Dograh configs = snake oil (prompts sold as "custom")
From ISABELLA's POV:  Zapier-only solutions = snake oil (no-code sold as "automation")
From ZAPIER's POV:    Manual processes = snake oil (humans sold as "systems")
From MANUAL's POV:    Nothing exists below them (they're the floor)
```

You don't need to prove something IS objectively bad. You just need to show — from YOUR offer's capabilities — why the alternative is incomplete. Then their customers become yours.

## The Process

1. **Identify** what looks like snake oil from your offer's perspective
2. **Articulate** specifically WHY (not "they suck" — "they can't do X because Y")
3. **Educate** the prospect on the difference (this is the PSA / SCREENING)
4. **Demonstrate** your offer doing what theirs can't (this is the DOCTOR diagnostic)
5. **Steal** their customers by showing the gap (they come to you, you don't chase)

## The Litmus Test (from SCREENING)

"Ask your current provider: can you redevelop the underlying platform with LiveKit if we need custom builds? How long will that take and what will it entail?"

If they say "hooking up APIs" — that's your snake oil signal.

## Why This Isn't Mean

You're not lying. You're not trashing people. You're EDUCATING the market about what's real and what's incomplete. The prospect deserves to know the difference. If their current provider can't do what you can, that's a FACT, not an insult.

## The Scrooge McDuck Principle

Once you identify the snake oil layer below you, those customers are PRE-QUALIFIED. They already WANT the thing — they just got the wrong version. Your PSA lands perfectly because they already feel the pain of the incomplete solution. You're not selling them something new. You're UPGRADING them.
