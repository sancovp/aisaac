Concentration Engineering: How SOMA Makes AI Actually Focus

Every AI system has an attention problem. It drifts. It forgets what it was doing. It confuses activity with progress. We solved this with three mechanisms that didn't exist before today.

The Problem: AI Has ADHD

You give an AI agent a task. It starts working. Halfway through, it gets distracted by a tangent. It produces output that LOOKS productive but isn't the thing you asked for.

Nothing enforces what the AI reports about its own state. It can claim it's "focused on X" while actually doing Y. There's no measurement of concentration. No consequence for drift.

Solution 1: Persona Identity with CoR Templates

Every AI persona in SOMA has a Chain of Reasoning template — a set of blanks it fills every turn. A developer persona reports: what component, what rationale, what approach, what risk. A researcher reports: what hypothesis, what evidence, what confidence, what next step.

What you force the AI to report reveals whether it's actually thinking. If the "component" field contains something SOMA has never seen, a deduction chain flags it. If the "rationale" contains "always" or "never," the system suggests writing a rule. The template IS the enforcement.

Solution 2: Focus & Surfing

Focus is singular. Only one endeavor at a time. When you declare focus, SOMA tracks your surf streak — consecutive events without getting blocked. Switch focus? Old streak freezes. Get blocked by something only a human can answer? Wipeout. Streak resets.

This creates a concentration metric that's completely automatic. No self-reporting. No standups. The system KNOWS how concentrated you are because it measures it structurally.

The analytics accumulate: wipeout frequency, mean streak before wipeout, focus switch rate, best peak ever. Over time, you can see whether your concentration is improving or degrading — and the system tells you WHY (which blockers cause wipeouts most often).

Solution 3: Skills That Trigger Workflows

A skill in SOMA isn't just context — it's a workflow trigger. When you activate a skill that has an endeavor template, SOMA automatically opens that endeavor as your new focus. The expected steps are defined. Miss one? SOMA tells you. Do them out of order? SOMA tells you.

This means workflows enforce themselves. No manager needed. No checklist to forget. The skill IS the workflow, and activating it IS starting the workflow.

Solution 4: Self-Detection

The system detects its own gaps. When your CoR mentions a component SOMA has never observed, it says "observe it." When you mention fragility as a risk, it says "add a defensive chain." When you express an invariant in your rationale, it says "write a rule."

The system literally grows itself by detecting what's missing from its own knowledge. Every turn of work produces new detection of what the system should know but doesn't yet.

Why This Matters For Your Business

If your AI agents can't concentrate, they burn tokens without producing results. If they can't validate their own reasoning, they hallucinate confidently. If their workflows don't self-enforce, someone has to babysit them.

SOMA eliminates all three problems. Your agents declare what they're doing, prove they're doing it correctly, and follow workflows that enforce themselves. The result: AI that actually works autonomously without a human checking every output.

This is what L6 — Concentration Engineering — means. Not just building AI that does things. Building AI that stays focused on the right things and proves it every turn.
