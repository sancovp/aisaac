# Universal Course Template — "Teach-While-Showing-Your-System"

The meta-structure of a course that **sells a system by teaching the generic skill and showing your
advanced implementation at every step.** The teaching is the value; the *gap* between the generic way and
your-system's way is the funnel. (Abstracted from a 2h50m GTM-in-Claude-Code course; substrate-independent
— works for any domain where you have a generic skill + an opinionated advanced system.)

## The invariant shape

**0. OPEN — Hook + Promise.** `{transformation}` (where the learner ends up) · `{proof}` (outcomes/numbers,
honestly marked as claims) · `{core_thesis}` (the ONE idea the whole course orbits) · a free companion
artifact (`{lead_magnet_0}`) they can grab right now.

**1..N. MODULES — ordered beginner→advanced, two kinds INTERLEAVED:**
- **CONCEPT module** (teach one `{primitive}`). Beat:
  `CONCEPT → plain-word analogy → "but in MY {system} I do it like THIS" (cut to a real artifact) → the durable BENEFIT (ties back to {core_thesis}) → [funnel nudge]`
- **BUILD module** (live demo of one `{build}`). Beat:
  `goal/docs → plain-language architecture → diagram → plan/grill (stress-test the plan) → iterate/anneal against ground truth → ship/deploy`

**Special modules (fixed positions in the arc):**
- **THESIS module** (early) — establish `{core_thesis}` as the lens everything after is seen through.
- **HIGHEST-LEVERAGE-SYSTEM module** (mid-late) — the thing that turns *tools* into a *system* (the
  feedback / self-improvement loop). *"Without it, a tool; with it, a system."*
- **CAPSTONE BUILD** (late) — the big end-to-end thing that PROVES the whole course and lands the one hard CTA.
- **ARCHITECTURE / OS module** — zoom out: the whole system as one picture (state, stages, durability).
- **CLIMAX / DIFFERENTIATOR module** (end) — the deepest thing, positioned as *"the most important thing"* — your true moat.

## The two beat patterns (the per-module engine)
- **Concept beat:** primitive → plain analogy → *how I do it in my system* (real artifact) → the forever-benefit → funnel nudge.
- **Build beat:** docs/goal → plain architecture → diagram → plan/grill → anneal-against-ground-truth → ship.
(Concept modules teach; build modules prove. Interleave so a concept is demonstrated soon after it's taught.)

## The funnel, woven in (not bolted on)
- **Lead magnets throughout** — free artifacts (repo, CLIs, data, free chapters) = *permissionless value*; the teaching itself IS the magnet.
- **ONE hard CTA, at the capstone** (where desire peaks): "book a call / buy the thing."
- **Upsell teases AFTER the capstone** (in the advanced module): name the higher tiers without pitching.
- **The product is the `{system}` you showed at every concept beat** — the learner has watched it solve every problem; the gap sells itself. (Demonstration-through-execution: show, never pitch.)

## The threaded theses
A few core ideas (`{core_thesis}` + 2–4 supporting) repeated in EVERY module's benefit beat. The repetition is the conditioning — by the end they believe the thesis because they saw it pay off N times.

## Variables (fill these to instantiate any course)
`{transformation}` · `{proof}` · `{core_thesis}` + `{supporting_theses[]}` · `{primitives[]}` (ordered
beginner→advanced) · `{system}` (your advanced implementation, shown at each concept beat) · `{builds[]}`
(the live demos, interleaved) · `{highest_leverage_system}` · `{capstone_build}` · `{climax_differentiator}`
· `{lead_magnets[]}` · `{hard_CTA}` · `{upsells[]}`.

## Why it works
The learner came for the generic skill — that's real value, freely given. But at every step they watch your
system do the same thing *better and permanently*. You never pitch; the **gap between "how you'd do it" and
"how the system does it forever" is the entire sales argument**, made by demonstration. By the capstone they
want the system, and the one CTA converts desire that the course itself manufactured. Composes with:
`aida-fractal-value-ladder` (each module is an AIDA cycle), the framework CHAPTER format (each module ≈ a
chapter), `autorealization-and-progressive-disclosure` (the learner climbs a gradient module by module).
