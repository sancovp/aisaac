### Key Contradictions Between Sources

1. **Agency model viability:** Puru is bullish (teaches it, charges $10K-$15K/project). Mark says B-tier and "window closing." Olly says "fulfillment is easy, acquisition is hard." The truth is likely: agencies work NOW but the window is narrowing as Claude Co-Work democratizes what agencies sell.

2. **SaaS viability:** Olly is bullish on API-first SaaS. Mark says SaaS window has passed UNLESS you target non-technical demographics. Both are right: traditional dashboard SaaS is dying; API-first SaaS consumed by agents is the evolution.

3. **Community model:** Puru uses Skool successfully (18K members). Mark warns that low-ticket MRR communities have brutal churn and require genuine expertise. The reconciliation: Skool works as TOP of funnel (free tier), not as the revenue engine itself.

### The Meta-Pattern

Every source converges on one insight: **the build is commoditized; the distribution is the business.** Whether it is an API, an app, an agency, or a cohort -- AI tools make building trivially easy. The competitive advantage is: (a) picking the right market, (b) crafting the right message, (c) reaching the right people, (d) retaining them through genuine value that AI alone cannot replicate.

### Universal Pricing Principles (Cross-Source)

1. **Price higher than feels comfortable.** Every source says this. New founders always underprice.
2. **Revenue IS validation.** A paid customer > trial signup > poll response > interest (Olly).
3. **Trials are NOT validation.** Riddled with failed payments, fake cards, refunds. Hard paywalls give real signal (Olly for apps, general principle).
4. **Never go cheap.** $7/mo product = "worst nightmare" (Olly). Half churn from failed cards.
5. **Know your unit economics BEFORE affiliates.** 30% lifetime commission can make customers unprofitable if margins are thin.
6. **Annual lock-in is critical.** Steep annual discounts lock revenue and improve cash flow.
7. **Retention offers on cancellation.** One-time discounted annual shown only when cancelling = pure profit per saved subscriber (Olly for apps, applicable everywhere).

### Isaac's Pricing Position

Isaac's current pricing ($2K setup + $200/mo, $1K/mo retainer, $2.5K pre-built, $5K/mo custom) is **well-positioned** against this landscape:
- Higher than commodity SaaS ($30-$129/mo) but justified by compound intelligence
- Lower than Puru's agency model ($10K-$15K/project) -- potentially **underpriced** for the value
- The retainer model ($1K/mo) with "I'll give you the voice agent free" hook is smart pricing psychology
- Opportunity: add annual lock-in discounts to retainer ($1K/mo -> $8K/year)

## 6. Frameworks & Mental Models

### Auto Research Pattern (Nick Puru / Karpathy)
```
READ -> CHANGE ONE THING -> TEST -> SCORE -> IMPROVE? COMMIT : REVERT -> LOOP
```
- Three requirements: something to change, a number to track, a way to test
- Your competitor runs 30 tests/year. You run 30/day. You win by December.
- Cost: ~$0.24 for 5 optimization rounds
- Maps to Isaac's L4 (Harnesses) and the compound intelligence thesis

### Belief Chain Method (Mark Builds Brands)
- Identify max 6 beliefs customer must hold before purchasing
- Structure as "I believe that..." statements
- Advertorial's job is to install these beliefs sequentially
- Copy is the ARGUMENT for each belief, not hype
- Maps to Isaac's L1-L7 ladder: each level installs a new belief about AI's real capabilities

### Four Business Variables (Mark)
```
MARKET (most powerful) > PRODUCT > ADS > FUNNEL
```
- Market: urgency + high pain + high TAM
- Product: solves problem, $30+ margin, 3x COGS
- Ads: find winning angle (emotional reason people buy)
- Funnel: advertorial warms cold traffic before sales page

### Automation Priority Matrix (Nick Puru)
Score every recurring workflow on: hours/week consumed, revenue impact, feasibility. Top 3 = what you build first. Takes 1 hour. Prevents building cool but low-impact automations.

### SFM (Self-Fulfilling Lead Magnet) (Olly)
Free resource that automatically filters for your buyer persona. The lead magnet IS the qualification mechanism.

### The "Reveal" Framework (Olly for apps)
The app shows the user something about themselves they did not know. If someone can screen-record 10 seconds and people stop scrolling, you have a business.

### Demographic Arbitrage (Mark)
Sell AI-powered products/services to people who do not know AI exists yet. The tech bubble is tiny. Most of the world still does not know what Claude Code is. Shopify has been around since 2017 and most people STILL do not understand it.

### The Compounding Stack (Nick Puru)
```
New skill + existing connectors = new automated workflow
New connector + existing skills = existing workflows gain new reach
Model upgrade + existing system = everything improves automatically
```
Threshold: ~3-4 weeks of consistent building. Most people quit before compounding kicks in.

### Commoditization Curve (Mark)
```
Early (window open) -> Growth (window narrowing) -> Mature (window closed)
```
Current: TikTok Shop S->A, SMMA deep C, copywriting B->C, AI agency heading B->C.

### SaaS-to-Agent Shift (Puru / YC thesis)
```
SaaS: Tool helps humans work faster. Revenue = seats x price/seat.
Vertical AI Agent: System replaces human workflow. Revenue = outcomes x price/outcome.
```
$300B+ market opportunity. AI agents eliminate both software costs AND labor costs simultaneously.

### Three-Criteria Feature Filter (Olly / 37 Signals "Rework")
Only build a feature if it meets ALL THREE: (1) Multiple users asking (demand), (2) Gets user to win faster (impact), (3) Aligns with core mission. Otherwise: "We'll work on this after our current roadmap."

### API-as-Default-Tool (Olly)
In 2026, AI agents consume APIs, not dashboards. API stickiness (code-level lock-in) > SaaS stickiness (UI habit). Revenue scales with usage, not seats.

### "One App" Rule (Olly)
Build ONE thing. Not two, not five. ONE. Serious money comes from staying with one product long enough to understand its financials. Every switch restarts the learning curve.
