## Core Thesis

AI's real competitive advantage is NOT time savings or task automation -- it's **speed to acquire data**. The feedback loop (try -> get data -> revise -> repeat) that used to take weeks now takes hours. You can go from idea to live business in under 24 hours.

## The Four Business Variables Framework

Every business has exactly four variables you must get right:

### 1. MARKET (Most Powerful Lever)

Market selection is the #1 lever. "Stupid people make loads of money because they entered the right market at the right time."

**Three criteria for a great market:**

1. **Urgency** ("Market/Audience Urgency") - How quickly do people want their problem solved? Neuropathy device >> fishing gear. Physical urgent pain makes people act irrationally and buy fast.

2. **High Pain** - Can be physical OR emotional. Pet market example: people would rather suffer themselves than watch their pet suffer. Emotional pain counts.

3. **High TAM** (Total Addressable Market) - "Riches are in the niches is BS." 9-figure brands all enter massive markets: weight loss, pet supplements, Ozempic/semaglutide. Don't make market size a variable working against you.

**Good market examples:** Pet supplements, weight loss, hair growth, supplements, skincare, cosmetics, beauty, health & wellness.

**The Three Tests (from his mentor):** Sell something that does one of three things:
- Teaches them to **make more money**
- Teaches them to **have more sex**
- Teaches them to **lose more weight**

**Time allocation:** Market selection should take under 30 minutes. Don't overthink it.

### 2. PRODUCT (Not King -- But Not Nothing)

**Critical reframe:** Product is NOT the most important thing. Mark uses an audio EQ metaphor:
- If product is 10/10, your ads and funnel can suck and you still win (rare/lucky)
- If product is 5/10, you MUST compensate with better ads and better funnel
- Most people blame the product when their ads/funnel actually suck
- Ads and funnels are easier to fix than finding a self-selling product

**Three criteria for a solid product:**

1. **Solves a problem in a passionate market** (covered by market selection)
2. **Margins:**
   - Rule 1: At least **$30 gross margin** (sell for $30+ above sourcing cost)
   - Rule 2: **3x cost of goods sold** (a $100 product should sell for $300)
3. **Fits in a shoe box** (avoids shipping/logistics nightmares)

**AI for product finding:** Uses Claude Desktop "Co-work" feature with a custom Product Finder SOP (project instructions). You fill in a brief:
- Market
- Exclusions (e.g., no supplements)
- Budget to start
- Skill level (ads, funnels, newbie)
- Time horizon (quick flip vs. long-term brand)

Claude outputs competitor analysis with traffic data. Example products found:
- Jaw tension supplement for TMJ ($10M competitor in 2024)
- Perimenopause brain supplement (300K+ visits/mo competitor)
- Senior dog non-slip paw grips (insane margins, fits in shoebox)

### 3. ADS (Finding Your Winning Sales Message)

**Goal of first ads is NOT to make money** -- it's to **buy data** and find your **winning sales message**.

**Core concept: Angles.** Different people buy the same product for different reasons. Each reason = a different advertising angle. You need to find which angle resonates most.

**Strategy: Start with image ads** because:
- Extremely easy to create
- Can make hundreds in under an hour
- Fast testing = fast data

**AI process for angles:**
1. Go to Claude, create new project "AI Angles"
2. Paste SOP (provided in video description)
3. Fill in: product name, description, primary competitor angle
4. Claude outputs multiple angles with emotional hooks

**Example angles for dog paw grips:**
- Post-surgery/injury recovery: "The rehab tool vets are already using"
- Neurological/special needs dogs: "Finally something that works for his condition"
- New hardwood floors
- Floor anxiety/confidence loss: "He won't even come greet me at the door anymore"

**Image ad type: Native ads** - Designed to look like real Facebook posts (not ads). Emotional, storytelling-based copy. Uses AI-generated images via Higgs Field (Nano Banana Pro model).

### 4. FUNNEL (The Chad Funnel Structure)

**Standard (bad) funnel:**
```
Image Ad -> Sales Page (PDP) -> Checkout
```
This is like asking a stranger to go home with you. Too abrupt.

**"Chad" funnel (advertorial model):**
```
Image Ad -> Pre-Lander (Advertorial) -> Sales Page -> Order Page
```

**What is an advertorial?** Ad + Editorial combined. Sells subconsciously without feeling like a sales pitch.

**Why it works:**
- Native image ads get extremely low cost traffic (cold traffic)
- Cold traffic needs warming up before seeing a sales page
- Advertorial leverages fundamental human psychology
- Sells subconsciously = higher conversion rate on colder traffic
- Net result: cheaper clicks + better conversion = more profit

**AI process for advertorial:**
1. Claude project with SOP (link in description)
2. Attach foundational docs (customer research)
3. Upload a competitor advertorial swipe (proven structure)
4. Claude rewrites based on your brand
5. Copy chief the output (review/refine copy)
6. Add images (created in Higgs Field, purpose-driven)
7. Output is HTML -- host on Shopify, ClickFunnels, Funnelish, CloudFlare, anywhere

**Time:** ~4.5 minutes to generate a full advertorial.

**Image rule for landers:** Every image must serve a purpose. It should either:
1. **Install a belief**, OR
2. **Overcome an objection**

Never put images just to make it look pretty.

## Key Actionable Insights

1. **Speed is the moat.** AI compresses feedback loops from weeks to hours. The person who tests fastest wins.
2. **Market > Product > Ads > Funnel** in terms of leverage, but all four must be at least "good enough."
3. **Don't blame the product.** Fix your ads and funnel first -- they're easier to improve.
4. **$30 gross margin minimum, 3x COGS** -- non-negotiable pricing rules.
5. **Native image ads + advertorial funnel** is the specific playbook for cold traffic conversion.
6. **Angles are the secret.** Find the emotional reason people buy, not just the functional benefit.
7. **Claude Co-work + SOPs** is the actual AI workflow -- not just chatting with AI, but structured project-based prompting.
8. **Buy data, not sales** initially. First ad spend is research, not revenue.
