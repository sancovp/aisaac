## Key Tactics (with quotes)

### 1. The Anti-Sales-Call Discovery: Boot Camp > Discovery Calls

Isabella's defining moment was learning that 200 sales calls converted only 2 deals ($40K total), while a single boot camp system generated $150K with less work.

> "We did like 200 calls and we were back to back to back for like two, three weeks... I think we closed two deals... $40,000 from all of that work. But then I said, you know what, there's a better way... that whole system it generated us $150,000 with like not even that same amount of work."

The boot camp is a **$47 virtual event**, 3 days, 1 hour per day. On day 3 she pitches a high-ticket offer ($3K-$10K). The boot camp replaces the discovery call entirely — it IS the discovery call, but one-to-many instead of one-to-one.

> "It's essentially doing one sales call for multiple people all at once, so that I don't have to then go on multiple sales calls one by one and pitch the same thing over and over again."

### 2. The "$47 First Dollar" Psychology

Her entire system is built on one insight: getting the first dollar is the hardest; the second is 90% easier.

> "There's a psychological factor in sales. Getting the first dollar out of your clients is always like the hardest, but getting the second is usually 90% easier."

The $47 boot camp IS the first dollar. Once they pay $47, converting them to $8K is dramatically easier because:
- They self-selected as willing to pay
- They spent 3 days with Isabella (trust built)
- They saw the value firsthand
- The pitch happens in a context of education, not cold selling

### 3. LinkedIn-First Personal Brand (60-30-10 Content Formula)

Isabella chose LinkedIn over Instagram/TikTok because her ICP (business owners and professionals) lives there. She grew to 47K followers on LinkedIn with this content mix:

- **60% pure value:** How-to posts, frameworks, breakdowns, funnel deep dives
- **30% story and lessons:** Her entrepreneurial journey, wins, losses
- **10% results and proof:** Case studies, client wins, specific numbers

Additional content rules:
- **1-1-1 formula:** One problem, one solution, one ideal client, one offer. Focus until $1M.
- **Strong hooks** in first two lines (or people scroll past)
- **Never share links in posts** — tell people to comment instead (avoids LinkedIn throttling)
- **Carousels with QR codes** — high engagement format, sneaks offer onto page 2 via QR code
- **No paid ads, no cold outreach, no SEO** — 100% organic LinkedIn content

> "I built a million dollar business just by doing none of that [paid ads, cold outreach, SEO]."

### 4. Lead Magnets That Do Market Research

Her lead magnets serve double duty — they generate leads AND feed her marketing copy:

**BANT questionnaire on opt-in:** Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline. Qualifies leads automatically during download.

**"What's your #1 challenge?" question:** When in discovery mode, she replaces BANT with a single open-ended question. Responses feed directly into content and marketing copy.

> "People will tell you, oh, it's the tech, it's this, it's that. So then that feeds my marketing material. When I post content... I use their language."

She uses **links, not PDFs** so she can track time spent on resources. She tracks BANT responses to score leads.

Lead magnets around AI agents alone generated **$180K in 6 weeks** via the boot camp system.

### 5. No-Sales-Call Digital Sales Process

Isabella eliminated sales calls entirely through a "buyers enablement" approach:

**Deal rooms** — a comprehensive document/page containing:
- Value proposition
- Their problems and why this is the solution
- Examples of how she solved it
- An agent they can play around with
- AI chatbot trained as a "ninja salesperson" that qualifies while answering questions

> "How do we enable our buyers to buy from us because buying has changed. Now people know that when they get on a call, they're going to be sold."

When someone insists on a call, she handles it via email first. If a call still happens, it's 15 minutes max — just logistics and final trust confirmation, NOT objection handling.

> "The people have already self-selected, they already know it's for them, and it's just a matter of like I just want to make sure you're a real person."

### 6. Pricing Model and Value Ladder

Her ladder is explicit and well-structured:

| Tier | Price | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Boot camp | $47 | 3-day virtual training, 1hr/day |
| Boot camp upsell | $199-$299 | Immediate cart value bump |
| High-ticket backend | $3K-$10K | "Let me build your agent/app for you" |
| Recurring maintenance | $500-$2K+/mo | Agent maintenance and support |
| Retreat/Mastermind | Premium (multi-K) | In-person, private chef, tight group |
| Growth Partnership | $10K-$20K/mo + rev share | Max 10 at a time, yearly contracts, extension of their team |

The key insight on growth partnerships:

> "Instead of just being another $2,000 a month agency, now you're actually charging $10,000 a month, $20,000 a month, but you're also getting revenue share because these are like long-term business partnerships."

She caps growth partnerships at 10 to maintain quality, and they are yearly contracts (not month-to-month).

### 7. Top 3 AI Agent Use Cases (What Sells)

1. **Speed to Lead** — AI calls back ad opt-ins instantly instead of waiting 48 hours. Easy upsell for ad agencies.
2. **AI Receptionist** — Answers inbound calls, routes to right department. Start with top 3-5 FAQs, not the whole knowledge base. "Minimum viable agent."
3. **After Hours Agent** — Handles calls outside business hours, holidays, weekends. Service businesses love this.

### 8. Earned Media Through Content (Not PR)

Isabella was featured in Forbes 4 times and Business Insider — all earned, not paid.

> "I have been featured on Forbes like four different times and Business Insider and a bunch of different publications where it was all earned media. They came to me."

Her content has been seen 8M+ times. Her strategic partner network reaches 15M people through content sharing.

### 9. "Productize Your AI Agents"

Her framing of the market opportunity:

> "The biggest gold rush is when you productize your AI agents and your AI employees. Analysts estimate a multi-trillion dollar services market is becoming productized."

This is not just "build agents for clients" — it is "package your agent into a repeatable product that scales without you."

### 10. Case Studies Without Clients

For breaking into new markets, she uses analysis of OTHER companies as case studies:

> "You don't necessarily have to start with like your own success stories. When I was trying to break into the SaaS space... I did case studies on like other companies and other tools, and it was just my analysis of like how I saw it work. And that's enough to also build trust until you get your own case studies."
