## Framework: The Boot Camp Model

**What it is:** A $47 virtual event that replaces discovery calls and sells one-to-many instead of one-to-one.

**Structure:**
- **Price:** $47 per attendee (low-ticket entry)
- **Duration:** 3 days, 1 hour per day
- **Content:** Teaches how to go from point A to point B on a specific challenge
- **Purpose:** Education layer that warms leads from content into buyers
- **Day 1-2:** Training + Q&A (builds trust, connection, demonstrates expertise)
- **Day 3:** Pitch the high-ticket offer ($3K-$10K)
- **Upsell at checkout:** $199-$299 add-on to increase average cart value
- **Frequency:** Once a month (or every 3 weeks)
- **Variations tested:** VIP tier, general tier, add-ons — constantly A/B testing pricing

**The Psychology:**
"Getting the first dollar out of your clients is always the hardest, but getting the second is usually 90% easier."

The boot camp gets the first dollar ($47), builds 3 days of trust and Q&A (equivalent to doing ONE sales call for ALL attendees at once), and then the day 3 pitch is a no-brainer because they already know they have a problem and you're the trusted provider standing in front of them.

**Numbers:**
- Old way: 200 calls, 2 closes, $40K
- Boot camp way: $150K from same audience with less work
- AI agents boot camp series: $180K in 6 weeks
- Sold $8K agent builds straight off the website after boot camp — no sales calls

**Show-up rate hack:** AI voice agent calls bootcamp participants 15 minutes before the event. Increased show-up rate from 40% to 63%.

**How to apply to TWI/AIsaac:**
This is the single most applicable framework. Isaac's planned cohort at $997 is too high for a first-dollar capture. The play:
1. Run a $47 "AI for Your Business in 3 Days" boot camp monthly
2. Upsell a $199 VIP tier at checkout (recordings + templates + private Q&A)
3. Day 3: pitch the $2,500 pre-built voice agent system
4. Those who buy the agent become candidates for $1K/mo retainer
5. This replaces cold calls entirely — content drives to boot camp, boot camp sells

Isaac's voice agent can call registrants 15 min before each session (dogfooding the product while improving show rates).

## Framework: The Deal Room (Buyer Enablement)

**What it is:** A self-serve sales environment that replaces the discovery call for high-ticket offers.

**Structure:**
A deal room is a page (PDF or dedicated tool) containing EVERYTHING the buyer needs to make a decision without talking to anyone:
- Value proposition (clear, specific to ONE ideal client)
- Their problems (named explicitly)
- Why this is the solution
- How you solve it (process/methodology)
- Examples/case studies of how you've solved it before
- Interactive element (e.g., a demo agent they can play with)
- AI chatbot trained as a "ninja salesperson" — answers questions AND qualifies ("Tell me more about your business")

**Key Concept — Buyer Enablement vs. Seller Enablement:**
"Up until now we've all been in a sellers enablement market. Two years ago things started shifting where now it should be buyers enablement. How do we enable our buyers to buy from us because buying has changed. People know that when they get on a call, they're going to be sold. People hate getting on calls."

**Critical constraint:** Must be hyper-focused on ONE ideal client. If you're selling to gym owners AND realtors AND brokers, the deal room won't work because you can't lay everything out specifically enough.

**How she handles call requests:**
- If someone still wants a call: sends email asking "Is there anything specific you want to talk about?"
- Tries to resolve over email first
- If call is unavoidable: max 15 minutes, just alignment/logistics, not objection handling
- "Those 15 minutes is really just to like get alignment, solidify the trust"

**How to apply to TWI/AIsaac:**
Isaac needs a deal room page for each offer tier. The voice agent page on the site should BE the deal room: specific problem (losing after-hours leads), specific solution (AI voice agent), specific proof (demo agent embedded on page), specific price, and a chatbot that qualifies + answers questions. The chatbot IS the product demo AND the sales rep simultaneously.

## Framework: BANT Scoring (Lead Qualification)

**What it is:** Standard sales qualification framework repurposed as opt-in form questions.

**Structure:**
- **B**udget — Do they have money to spend?
- **A**uthority — Are they the decision maker?
- **N**eed — Do they actually need this?
- **T**imeline — When do they need a solution?

**How she uses it:**
- Embeds BANT questions in the lead magnet opt-in form
- When in "discovery mode" (learning market language), she swaps BANT for: "What's your number one challenge with [subject] right now?"
- Uses their language back in marketing material: "If you're dealing with tool overwhelm, here's five steps..."
- Tracks time spent on resources (uses links not PDFs so she can measure engagement)

**Two modes:**
1. **Qualification mode:** BANT questions at opt-in to score leads
2. **Discovery mode:** Open-ended challenge question at opt-in to learn market language

**How to apply to TWI/AIsaac:**
Isaac's lead capture form should include BANT-style questions, not just email. Even a simple "What's your biggest challenge with AI right now?" feeds marketing language directly. The responses become blog topics, email subjects, and ad copy. This is the discovery flywheel — leads tell you what to say to attract more leads.
