## Key Frameworks & Insights

### 1. The "Reveal" Framework (Idea Selection)

**The #1 reason people fail is picking the wrong idea, not the build.**

- Search TikTok for "app demos" with 500K+ views
- Study what happens in the first 3 seconds of viral app videos
- The pattern: the app shows the user something about themselves they didn't know or were afraid of
- Examples: face rating, body scan, calorie count from photo, height prediction, personality score, IQ test, prayer consistency tracker
- **The reveal IS your entire marketing strategy.** If someone can screen-record 10 seconds of your app and people stop scrolling to watch, you have a business. If not, you'll pay for every download through ads.

**Emotional Triggers That Print Money (permanent human insecurities):**
- Attractiveness / appearance
- Health / fitness / weight
- Intelligence / brain training
- Status / charisma / social skills
- Fear of being left behind
- Spiritual discipline / religious consistency

These are NOT trends. They've been selling products for centuries. TikTok just made distribution free.

**Ethical Note from Olly:** He personally recommends building apps that help people (brain training, book summaries, meditation) rather than ones that prey on appearance insecurity. "Just for your soul."

### 2. Competitive Gap Analysis

- Download the top 5 apps in your niche
- Go through each onboarding as a real user, screenshot every screen
- Pay attention to when you feel pulled forward vs. when you feel like leaving
- Read App Store reviews: what people love AND what they complain about
- **The gap between what users love and what frustrates them is where your angle lives**
- You don't need to invent something new. Build the same thing for a more specific audience or with a stronger personality.
- Example: Prayer Lock was built off Pray Screen. Step Lock combined walking apps with screen time tools.

### 3. The "One App" Rule

**Build ONE app. Not two, not five. ONE.**

- Serious money comes from staying with one app long enough to understand its financials
- Every time you switch apps you restart the learning curve (cost per download, conversion rates, etc.)
- You'll never get marketing momentum if you're constantly switching projects

### 4. The Build (Trivial)

- Use AI tools: **Rork** or **Cursor** with **React Native**
- Describe your app in extreme detail (not "I want a fitness app" but the full onboarding flow, paywall, core feature)
- Expect 5-10 iterations before something usable
- First week goal: complete flow from launch -> onboarding -> paywall that doesn't crash
- Ship core feature + onboarding + paywall. Nothing else. Polish later.
- Pay $99/year for Apple Developer Program BEFORE building (identity verification takes 1-2 days)
- Apply for Apple's Small Business Program immediately: reduces Apple's cut from 30% to 15% (for apps under $1M/year revenue)

### 5. Onboarding as Product (The 3-Act Structure)

**"Your onboarding IS your product."** This is where conversions happen or don't.

Referenced: A fasting app with a 67-step onboarding. People complete it because they care.

**Act 1 -- Establish the Problem:**
- Don't tell users they have a problem. Ask questions that make them say it themselves.
- Example: "How often do you [habit] per week?" -> "How long per session?" -> "That's 365 hours per year."
- This is not manipulation. It's making the user reflect.
- After every 3-4 questions, mirror their answers back: "Based on what you've shared, your biggest challenge is staying consistent without accountability."
- This is why users don't bounce during long onboardings -- it feels personal, built specifically for them.

**Act 2 -- Let Them Use the Core Feature:**
- Don't describe what the app does. Make the user DO it.
- Face rating app -> let them take a photo. Food scanner -> let them scan something. Prayer tracker -> let them set up their first reminder.
- The moment they experience the core feature = the moment they understand the value.
- **Show the review prompt RIGHT HERE** -- at peak excitement. Not after they pay, not 3 days later. NOW.
- A 4-star app with 500 reviews outperforms a 5-star app with fewer reviews.

**Act 3 -- The Paywall:**
- User should have spent 8-12 minutes in onboarding before hitting the paywall
- **Sunk cost fallacy**: people don't abandon something they've invested time in
- **Hard paywall, no free trial.** Trials pull in users who never pay and inflate download numbers while revenue stays flat.
- You want a real purchase decision. If people pay, you have a real business. If they defer and cancel, you have a problem.

**Resource:** Mobbin (mobbin.com) -- shows onboarding flows of top apps like Headspace, Airbnb, Mercury.

### 6. Pricing Strategy

**Price higher than feels comfortable.** Every successful founder says this. New founders always underprice.

**Working pricing model:**
- $9/week (anchor price)
- $49/year (what you actually want them to take)
- Both shown on paywall -- annual looks reasonable compared to weekly

**Why it works:** The user who downloaded after seeing a TikTok video is emotionally motivated, not casually browsing. After 8-12 minutes of onboarding, they're far more motivated than a typical app user. They WILL pay.

**Retention offer on cancellation:**
- One-time discounted annual subscription ($34-$44)
- Shown only when they initiate cancellation
- Apple permits this specific implementation
- Every saved churning subscriber is pure profit

**Target: Lifetime value per paying user above $30.** This number is what everything else gets measured against.

### 7. Content Marketing (Before Ads)

**Start with free content. This is not optional, even if you have money.**

- Post on TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts -- cross-post same video to all three
- Aim for 7 posts per day across platforms combined (~2-3 original videos cross-posted)
- **Volume > quality at this stage.** You're looking for feedback on angles and formats.
- Tool recommendation: **Reel Farm** for creating content

**Best-performing format:**
- Reaction clip + demo stitch
- Buy short reaction clip from creator OR use AI avatars
- App demo should be max 8 seconds
- **The hook (text overlay in first 2 seconds) is the most important part** -- spend more time on hook than footage
- Study competitors' most-viewed videos. Copy the structure (hook style, pacing, ending), not the content.

**Critical rule:** Do NOT touch your app based on what happens during this content phase. Don't add features, change colors, tweak onboarding. The only variable worth changing is content. Keep posting until something hits.

### 8. Paid Acquisition (After Organic Proof)

**Only run paid ads AFTER organic content proves it can get downloads.** Otherwise you're paying for information you could've gotten free.

**TikTok Ads Setup:**
- Smart Plus campaign
- Optimize for subscribe/purchase events, NOT installs (installs don't make money)
- Budget: ~$50/day
- Target: US only
- Run 2-3 days before drawing conclusions
- Creative: use your best-performing organic videos, add clear CTA
- **The ad should look like a regular TikTok, not a polished brand video.** The moment someone thinks "this is an ad," you've lost them.

**Three Numbers That Tell You If You Have a Winner:**
1. **Cost per Install (CPI):** target ~$2
2. **Click-Through Rate (CTR):** above 1%
3. **Cost per Acquisition (CPA):** must be below your LTV per user

**Scaling rules:**
- If all three metrics in range: increase budget by 30% every 3 days
- Do NOT increase faster (TikTok's algorithm resets and performance tanks)
- If not working after 2-3 days: kill the creative, make new ones. Don't change campaign structure. Problem is almost always creative, not targeting.

**Bonus:** TikTok matches your first $6,000 in ad spend on new accounts -- doubles your first month testing budget.

### 9. Post-Launch Iteration

- At 300+ downloads/day: go back and iterate onboarding properly
- Look where users drop off, kill screens not earning their place
- Test different paywall copy, different question order
- Target: download-to-trial conversion rate of at least 10%
- **Do NOT add new features until numbers force you to.** New features don't fix distribution problems -- they give you something to hide behind.
- Check App Store reviews regularly: same complaint 5+ times = fix it. Same praise 5+ times = double down on it.

## The Formula (TL;DR)

```
1. Find pain (TikTok research -> emotional triggers -> reveal moment)
2. Build ONE app with AI tools (Rork/Cursor + React Native, 2 weeks)
3. Design killer onboarding (3 acts: problem -> core feature -> hard paywall)
4. Price high ($9/week + $49/year, no free trial)
5. Content blitz (7 posts/day across TikTok/IG/YouTube, reaction+demo format)
6. Paid ads only after organic proof (TikTok Smart Plus, $50/day, optimize for purchases)
7. Iterate onboarding at 300+ downloads/day, never add features to fix distribution
```
