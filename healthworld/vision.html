👁️Vision & Hearing

Degradation is slow enough to normalize. That is the danger.

Risk signals

What to watch.

You adapt to declining vision and hearing without noticing. By the time you notice, significant capacity is already gone.

Screen distance creeping

Holding phone further away. Squinting at monitors. Gradual = easy to ignore.

Asking people to repeat

Especially in noisy environments. Early hearing loss signal.

Eye strain / headaches

End-of-day headaches from screen work. Prescription may have changed.

Night vision declining

Difficulty driving at night. Could be normal aging or early cataract.

Maintenance

The floor.

This is not aspirational. This is the minimum viable maintenance schedule. Everything above this is upside.

CadenceAction
Daily20-20-20 rule: every 20min, look 20ft away for 20 seconds.
WeeklyScreen brightness and ergonomic position audit.
AnnuallyEye exam. Hearing test after age 40 or if any signal appears.
As neededBlue light glasses if screen strain persists despite breaks.
Professional routing

When to call the specialist.

Optometrist (eyes) / Audiologist (ears)

Cadence: Annual eye exam. Hearing baseline at 40.

Trigger: Sudden vision change, tinnitus onset, ear pain, significant difficulty in noisy environments

Sudden vision changes (floaters, flashes, loss of peripheral vision) are medical emergencies. Do not wait for an appointment. ER.

Stat buffs

What you gain.

Maintaining this department doesn't just prevent failure. It compounds into measurable gains.

+Reading endurance +Driving safety +Social engagement +Cognitive load reduction

Make this department green.

Pick one signal. Make it visible. Track it for 30 days. That is the entire protocol.
