The origin

No CS degree. No bootcamp. No safety net.

I learned to code entirely through AI. When the AI failed, I couldn't just write it myself. That constraint meant every solution had to be architectural. I couldn't work around the hard parts. I had to solve them.

Two years later: a compound intelligence system that compounds intelligence. Every component added makes the next one smarter. The system builds skills, writes rules, accumulates knowledge, and improves while I sleep.

Most people building with AI are at layers 1-3. Prompts, context, tools. That's fine — it works. But the real leverage starts at layer 5: admissibility. That's where the system stops guessing and starts knowing. Nobody teaches that. I do.

The insight that changed everything: your mind has a 4-billion-token context window. An LLM has 200K. The game isn't AI vs human — it's AI composed with human. Your panoramic view + the AI's tireless keyhole = something neither could produce alone.

I call this holographic work. Every piece contains the whole. The frameworks are cognitive equipment AND operational skills. The automations are the frameworks running as code. The content is the frameworks explained to others.

Everything is the same thing at different levels of compilation.
