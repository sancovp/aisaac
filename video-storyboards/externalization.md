## Scene 2: The Lost Insight (0:15 - 1:15)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, empathetic] → [AI-IMAGE: A brain with dissolving edges where memories float away like sand in wind, contrasted with a crystalline external system where the same memories are preserved as solid glowing nodes]
**Voiceover:** "You had it. The connection between three ideas that had been bugging you for weeks. The solution crystallized — vivid, complete. Then something happened. A phone call. Sleep. Something trivial. Later, you remembered THAT you understood something. You couldn't remember WHAT. The label without the content. Those lost insights are gone permanently. Working memory holds 4 plus or minus 1 items. Short-term memory fades in hours. Long-term memory is reconstructive — you don't play back recordings, you reconstruct, and each reconstruction drifts."
**Remotion animation:** A brilliant insight crystallizing (glowing), then dissolving over time — the label remaining as a ghost while the content fades to nothing

## Scene 3: The Three Rules (1:15 - 2:45)
**Visual:** [AI-IMAGE: A split image - left side shows a person's head filled with tangled, fading thought threads; right side shows those same threads externalized into an organized glowing knowledge graph] → [AVATAR: GNOSYS counting]
**Voiceover:** "Three rules. Rule 1 — externalize before fade, not after. The capture window is short. If you think 'I'll write it down later' you're already losing it. Treat externalization as the last step of having the insight. Rule 2 — design for retrieval, not just storage. How will you find this? When will you need it? Storage without retrieval is storage without value. Rule 3 — external is default, internal is exception. The cost of unnecessary externalization is seconds. The cost of missed externalization is irreversible loss."
**Remotion animation:** Three rules appearing as golden commandments, each with a brief "right way" vs "wrong way" animation
