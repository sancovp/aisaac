## Scene 3: The Four Levels (1:30 - 3:00)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, teaching] → [REMOTION-ANIMATION: four levels stacking]
**Voiceover:** "Meta-cognitive awareness operates at four levels. Level 1 — State awareness. Knowing what cognitive and emotional state you're in right now. Heated? Flowing? Stuck? Reaching? Most people can't answer this at any given moment. Level 2 — Process awareness. Observing HOW you're thinking, not just what you're thinking about. Are you grinding linearly? Jumping between topics? Defending a position reflexively? Level 3 — Pattern recognition. Noticing recurring dynamics. 'I always drift at this point. I always white-screen after three hours.' Level 4 — Intervention capacity. Being able to ACT on what you observe. Detecting heat and invoking cooling. Catching drift and re-engaging mission. The meta is the step back — from being in the state to observing the state."
**Remotion animation:** Four transparent planes stacking vertically — state awareness at bottom, process awareness, pattern recognition, intervention capacity at top — each lighting up as described

## Scene 4: The Observer Stance (3:00 - 4:00)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, calm] → [AI-IMAGE]
**Voiceover:** "The key capacity is the observer stance. It's not dissociation — you're not disconnecting from experience. It's dual-track operation. You still feel the heat, the flow, the frustration. But simultaneously, a parallel process is watching. 'Ah, this is heat building. Level three. Attention starting to fragment.' It's experiencing AND observing the experience at the same time. This isn't mystical. It's trainable. And it's the operating system that every other framework runs on. Without it, your framework collection is a library nobody reads."
