## Scene 2: The Drift Problem (0:15 - 1:15)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, knowing] → [CUT TO: Excalidraw diagram]
**Voiceover:** "You start a work session with clear intentions. Build this feature. Ship this deliverable. First twenty minutes, locked in. Then you notice a code smell. Refactor it. Touches another file. Improve that too. Thirty minutes later you're deep in a rabbit hole that has nothing to do with the feature. You were busy the entire time. Productively busy. But the session failed. Because nobody was checking whether the work still served the mission. Any system without an alignment check will drift. The only question is how far."
**Remotion animation:** A straight arrow (mission) that starts curving gradually, then spiraling off into a completely different direction — without any dramatic moment, just gradual drift

## Scene 3: The Three Questions (1:15 - 2:45)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, authoritative] → [REMOTION-ANIMATION: three questions appearing]
**Voiceover:** "Mission Control asks three questions on a loop. Question 1: What am I doing right now? Not what I SHOULD be doing. What am I ACTUALLY doing. Most people can't answer this accurately because they've drifted without noticing. Question 2: Does this serve the mission? Direct connection to the stated goal. If you can't draw the line, you've drifted. Question 3: What should I do next? Based on the mission, not based on what's interesting. These three questions, asked every 25 minutes or on every state change, kill drift. Not with willpower. With protocol."
**Remotion animation:** Three questions appearing as a recurring checkpoint timer — every 25 minutes a "pulse" fires showing the three questions, the work re-aligns, then continues
