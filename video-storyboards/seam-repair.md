## Scene 2: The Break (0:15 - 1:15)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, diagnosing] → [CUT TO: Excalidraw diagram]
**Voiceover:** "You're deep in collaboration. Context is rich, mission clear. Then something shifts. Outputs stop matching. Your corrections don't stick. You're saying the same thing three ways and getting the same wrong result. First instinct: frustration. Second: say it louder. Third — if you're lucky — recognition. The seam broke. This isn't a competence problem. It's a coordination failure at the boundary between two contexts. That recognition changes everything. Because competence problems and coordination problems have completely different solutions."
**Remotion animation:** Two aligned panels (human and AI) drifting apart — a tear appearing in the seam between them, growing larger

## Scene 3: The Repair Protocol (1:15 - 2:45)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, hands-on repair mode] → [REMOTION-ANIMATION: repair sequence]
**Voiceover:** "Seam repair has a protocol. Step 1 — recognize the break. Stop pushing. The outputs aren't wrong because the AI is dumb. They're wrong because the contexts diverged. Step 2 — diagnose the divergence. When did it happen? What changed? What assumption broke? Step 3 — restore shared context. Re-state the mission explicitly. Re-establish what you're building and what success looks like. Step 4 — verify the repair. Check alignment before continuing. 'Are we on the same page now?' is not filler. It's structural verification. Step 5 — learn from the break. What caused it? Can you prevent it? Does the blanket need strengthening?"
**Remotion animation:** Two drifted panels being pulled back together — a repair stitch being applied, then a test pull to verify the seam holds
