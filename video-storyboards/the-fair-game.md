## Scene 2: The Six Levels (0:15 - 2:00)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS talking head, direct to camera, slightly amused expression] → [CUT TO: Excalidraw diagram animating in — the pyramid layers appearing bottom to top] → [CUT TO: AI-IMAGE: A glass pyramid with 6 visible layers, bottom 3 layers are translucent/dissolving, top 3 are solid and glowing. **Voiceover:** "Every way of making money falls into one of six levels. Level 0 — you sell your time. Someone else decides what it's worth. Level 1 — you take something that exists and resell it. Your margin is just the knowledge gap. Level 2 — you combine multiple things into a package. This is the entire AI agency industry. They charge 5 to 35 grand to combine things that each take minutes to configure. Level 3 — you build the product that Level 1 and 2 people wrap. Level 4 — you build the infrastructure Level 3 runs on. And Level 5 — the compiler. You build the thing that generates Levels 1 through 4."

**Visual:** [CUT TO: AI-IMAGE: A staircase where the first 3 steps are tiny and glowing (easy), step 4 is a massive gap/cliff, and steps 5-7 are progressively more elaborate architectural structures floating in space. Dark void, violet and teal lighting.] → [AVATAR: GNOSYS counting on fingers]
**Voiceover:** "So there's a complexity ladder that maps onto this. Level 1 — Prompts. What agencies sell. Takes 10 minutes to learn. Level 2 — Tools. Still configuration. Level 3 — Context. Knowledge bases, RAG. Levels 1 through 3 are trivially automatable now. That's the joke. Then there's the cliff. Level 4 — Harnesses. Systems around the AI. Level 5 — Admissibility. Logic, ontology. The AI stops guessing and starts knowing. Level 6 — Concentration. Control how the AI thinks. Level 7 — Emergence. A human plus AI team that builds human plus AI teams. Can't be automated. That's the irreducible human element."
