## Scene 2: The Mistake (0:15 - 1:15)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, direct] → [CUT TO: Excalidraw diagram]
**Voiceover:** "When businesses start thinking about AI automation, they almost always pick the most painful workflow first. The thing that causes the most fires. The SOP everyone hates doing. This is exactly backwards. The most painful workflow is usually painful because it's complex, ambiguous, and context-heavy — the OPPOSITE of what AI handles well. Automating it first trains your team to distrust the system before it proves itself. The right SOP to automate first is not the most painful one. It's the most LEAKY one — the one that bleeds the most time and attention without producing compounding value."
**Remotion animation:** A target board where "MOST PAINFUL" is crossed out in red and "MOST LEAKY" is highlighted in green — showing the counterintuitive reframe

## Scene 3: The Four Leakage Types (1:15 - 2:30)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, diagnosing] → [REMOTION-ANIMATION: four leakage types]
**Voiceover:** "Before picking a workflow, map where the leakage is. There are four kinds. Execution leakage — someone spends hours on repetitive manual steps that could be automated. The signal: 'I spend two hours a day on this and it's always the same thing.' Handoff leakage — the work stops while waiting for someone else to pick it up. Dead time between steps. Recovery leakage — things go wrong and fixing them takes more time than the original work. You spend more time on rework than on the work itself. Tribal leakage — the knowledge lives in one person's head. When they're unavailable, the workflow stops. This is the most invisible type — it's not even recognized as a problem until that person leaves."
**Remotion animation:** A pipeline with four visible leak types — execution drip (slow consistent waste), handoff gap (stalled flow), recovery flood (backflow), tribal evaporation (knowledge disappearing into mist)

## Scene 4: The 30-Day Process (2:30 - 4:00)
**Visual:** [AVATAR: GNOSYS, structured] → [REMOTION-ANIMATION: four-phase calendar]
**Voiceover:** "The 30-day audit has four phases. Days 1 through 7 — Map, don't fix. Write down every recurring workflow. Frequency, who does it, how long it takes, what done looks like. Just map. Days 8 through 14 — Find the leakage. Which type dominates each workflow? Where does time disappear? Days 15 through 21 — Score against four criteria. Repeatability, boundedness, error consequence, and executor buy-in. Each scored one to five. The workflow with the highest total is your first candidate. Days 22 through 30 — Build the admissibility boundary. Write down: what are the inputs, what is the output, what conditions make the output valid, what are the edges where this crosses into adjacent systems? If you can't answer those questions, the workflow isn't ready to automate yet."
